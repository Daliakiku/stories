lipgloss

I was sitting on my bed at home, my computer set on my legs. That house had a small balcony, and a flimsy plastic door that led outside. Rain was pounding on the air conditioner, hitting the tinny metal and filling the room with an overpowering kind of white noise. The smell of wet grass seeped in through the crack under the door. The mattress sagged with my weight. It had an indent down the middle, towards the top, where I always sat with my back against the wall doing homework. I was crying. I don’t remember why, but in that house I usually was.


My mom knocked on the wall and poked her head in through the open doorway. If she noticed the redness in my eyes, she didn’t say anything. She was probably used to it at that point.


“I’m getting rid of some of my makeup, do you want to look through to see if you want any?”


I looked at the small bamboo basket in her hands. I nodded my head slowly and she left the basket at the foot of my bed, then turned around and walked back out into the hallway, softly singing something under her breath. She always sings at full volume now. I leaned over and brought the basket towards me. She must have known that I never wore makeup. Maybe she was trying to tell me something. I wiped my eyes with the back of my hand. I turned the basket over and emptied everything out onto my bed. Almost everything in there looked old, from at least the 90s or early 2000s. I thought for a moment about how these tubes of lipstick are probably older than I was. I looked around my dimly lit room before getting up to close the door.


I think I still have the photos I took of myself after I finished painting my face. I had no idea what I was doing, and if I had let anyone see me before furiously wiping everything off they would have realized that. My cheeks were bright pink, a reddish kind of pink that I would never use nowadays. It doesn’t go with my skin tone. I had tried to encircle my eyes with eyeliner but it was smudged all over the edges where I had messed up and tried to wipe it with my fingers. I decided to draw two lines on the bridge of my nose, which I thought was very punk.


I dug through my closet, picked out my baggiest jeans and my biggest button-up shirt. There was nothing tight fitting in my closet, and nothing tight fitting would have fit me anyways. I stood in front of the mirror. I leaned in, staring myself in the eyes. I poked at my cheek, pulled on my lips. Tried pulling back the sides of my face to pretend I was skinny. I let go and I looked the same. I threw myself back onto my bed and stared at the ceiling. I could feel the ring of dried eyeliner around my eyes, and the mascara felt curiously heavy on my eyelashes. I laid there for a bit contemplating how I was going to get to the bathroom without anyone seeing me. I think I would die of embarrassment if my mom saw me right now. I still don’t really know why the thought of her seeing me with makeup on terrified me so much. She wore makeup every once in a while, even though she and I usually talked about how the beauty industry was a scam and pressuring women out of their money. Agreeing with her made me feel smart, so I agreed with her on everything.


Eventually I pushed myself out of bed and cracked the door open, slowly turning the doorknob so the lock wouldn’t click open. I slid my face into the crack, looking out with one eye and covering the rest of my face with my hand. My mom and dad were sitting on the couch watching something on the TV. I could probably make it to the bathroom without them seeing me. I opened the door slowly, and ran on my tip toes down the hall. When I reached the bathroom I clicked the door shut behind me, turning the lock. I knew there was a face wash somewhere in here, but wherever it was it was hidden away and obviously not used much. The good thing was that there wasn’t much space to search anyways.


I found it in the cabinet under the sink, tucked behind the yellowing pipes and countless plastic bottles of expired medicine. My mom always says that most expiration dates are there for decoration. I stood up, turning on the tap, and praying that whatever my parents were watching in the living room was loud enough to drown out the sound of the open faucet. I stood there scrubbing at my face for ten minutes at least, making sure there was no trace of makeup left. What I hadn’t realized at the time was that I had used waterproof mascara. Even now, I can’t ever seem to get it off in one wash, and now I actually know what makeup remover is.


Eventually I gave up. My face was left bright pink from all the scrubbing. I left the bathroom and sulked back to my room, laying face down on my bed. I left a strange imprint on my pillow from my still-wet face.


“Dinner’s ready guys!” My mom yelled from the living room.


I grabbed a lipgloss from my bed and carefully squeezed some out onto my lips. I guess I wanted to see what people would think if I stopped dressing like a boy for once. When I thought about it more, maybe my mom wasn’t as aggressively against makeup as I had convinced myself she was. And, it really wasn’t that big of a deal to wear lipgloss. The girls in my class did it all the time, and no one ever told them anything. I looked in the mirror again, and I liked my reflection a bit more than usual.


I sat down at the table next to my brother. My mom was sitting across from me, and she stayed looking at me for longer than usual. She didn’t say anything, but I could tell she was looking at my face, not my eyes.


“Are you wearing makeup?” She sounded quite surprised, considering she had just left me with a basket of makeup to choose from.


“Yeah…”


She laughed, but didn’t say anything else. My dad made some comment about how I was “all grown up now”. My face was bright red. My stomach started to hurt, and I lost my appetite. I poked at my food until everyone else was done eating, and when they started to clear the table I left my plate in the sink and went back to my room without saying anything. I dropped all the makeup back into the basket and hid it under my bed. I stopped for a second, took out the lipgloss, and stuffed it into the small pocket of my school bag before laying down on the carpet. Eventually I fell asleep.
